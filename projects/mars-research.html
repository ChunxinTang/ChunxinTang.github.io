Project Overview

Our Bass Connections team explored what it would take to establish a permanent settlement on Mars. We developed risk‚Äëbased decision scenarios for settlers and produced analysis and recommendations that drew on science, engineering, history, economics, ethics, law and international relations.

Research Focus Areas

The research focused on four critical dimensions of Mars settlement:

  • Habitats and Community Design: Architectural and social planning for sustainable living on Mars
  • Sustainability: Resource management and environmental considerations
  • International Relations and Security: Diplomatic frameworks and conflict resolution
  • Governance: Political structures and decision-making processes

Key Insights & Recommendations

  • Inclusive Governance: Emphasized the need for democratic and participatory decision-making structures
  • Sustainable Resource Management: Developed frameworks for equitable resource distribution and conservation
  • International Cooperation: Highlighted the importance of collaborative frameworks for space exploration
  • Risk Mitigation: Identified critical challenges including backward contamination and medical emergencies
  • Hidden Trade-offs: Revealed complex interdependencies between technical, social, and ethical considerations

Impact & Legacy

The project ran from summer 2020 to spring 2021 and led to the ongoing Duke Space Initiative. This experience sharpened my ability to evaluate complex, multidisciplinary problems at the intersection of technology, society and sustainability.
